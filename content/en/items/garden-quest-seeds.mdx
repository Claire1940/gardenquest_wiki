<Section icon={Target} title="Farming Setup: How to Plant Your First Garden Quest Seeds">

Succeeding in this experience requires a firm grasp of the core agricultural loop. When you first step onto your plot, the interface can feel overwhelming. However, by breaking down your daily routine into structured actions, you can maximize your space and ensure that your garden quest seeds are always working to generate passive income. 

The planting cycle is designed to be accessible yet strategically deep. You must balance the physical layout of your plots with the specific environmental needs of your crops. In the early stages, your primary bottleneck will be your manual watering speed and soil preparation efficiency.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Claim Your Plot and Clear Obstacles">
    Locate an open farming plot and claim it as your own. Use your basic starter tools to clear away weeds, stray rocks, and debris that block potential planting tiles.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Till the Soil and Prepare Beds">
    Equip your basic spade or hoe to till the cleared tiles. Properly tilled soil increases moisture retention, which directly influences the growth rate of your planted seeds.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Purchase and Plant Starter Seeds">
    Head to the local seed vendor and invest your starting coins into Turnip seeds. Equip the seeds from your inventory, aim at your tilled soil tiles, and plant them systematically.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Water and Monitor Growth Cycles">
    Fill your watering can at the nearest well or pond. Water each planted tile until the soil turns dark green. Keep an eye on the growth timer to ensure crops do not dry out.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Harvest and Reinvest Your Profit">
    Once the crops reach full maturity, harvest them to gain experience points and raw produce. Take your harvest to the market merchant, sell it for coins, and immediately reinvest in higher-tier seeds.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Water Efficiency Trick">
Do not wait for each crop tile to dry out completely before watering again. Maintaining a consistent moisture level of over fifty percent prevents growth stagnation and ensures your crops harvest exactly on schedule.
</Callout>

</Section>
